Purpose & How to Use This Report

Purpose of This Assessment

This assessment has been prepared to address a common challenge faced by property investors: making electrical decisions without clear visibility of actual risk.

  • Translate technical observations into investment-relevant risk insights
  • Distinguish between immediate safety concerns, long-term degradation, and non-issues
  • Support measured, non-reactive decisions aligned with property investment goals
  • Reduce unnecessary expenditure driven by uncertainty or conflicting advice

This assessment does not assume that all electrical deviations require rectification. Its primary role is to clarify what truly matters â€” and what does not.

How to Use This Report Effectively

  • Focus first on the Executive Summary and Risk Rating.
  • Use detailed sections to understand context, not to self-diagnose.
  • Treat recommendations as planning inputs, not mandatory instructions.

Scope, Limitations & Independence

Scope of Electrical Assessment

  • Main switchboard enclosure, layout, and protection devices (where accessible)
  • Circuit configuration and load distribution (where observable)
  • Presence and condition of RCD protection
  • General condition of visible cabling and terminations
  • Functional testing of selected outlets and lighting circuits
  • Earthing continuity at accessible points

Limitations of Assessment

This assessment is limited to accessible and visible areas only. Concealed wiring and inaccessible areas are excluded. These limitations are standard for non-invasive assessments and do not indicate any known defect.

General Observations

Installation Context

  • Partial upgrades suggest staged improvements over time.
  • No evidence of unsafe owner-performed modifications observed unless explicitly documented.

Operational Context

  • Electrical demand appears aligned with current occupancy profile under normal use.
  • No abnormal load behaviour noted during inspection unless explicitly documented.
